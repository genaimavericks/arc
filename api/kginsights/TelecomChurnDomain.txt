The dataset pertains to the domain of telecom customer churn, focusing on identifying factors that contribute to customers discontinuing their service. It comprises 14 records, each representing a unique customer identified by a CustomerID. The data includes a variety of features categorized as follows: demographic information (Gender, SeniorCitizen, Partner, Dependents), service usage details (PhoneService, MultipleLines, InternetService, OnlineSecurity, OnlineBackup, DeviceProtection, TechSupport, StreamingTV, StreamingMovies), 1  contractual and billing aspects (Contract, PaperlessBilling, PaymentMethod, MonthlyCharges, TotalCharges), and the target variable indicating churn (Churn). The data types of the features are a mix of categorical (represented as objects like 'Female', 'Yes', 'DSL', 'Month-to-month') and numerical (integers for SeniorCitizen and tenure, and floats for MonthlyCharges and TotalCharges). The Churn outcome is represented as a binary variable ('Yes' or 'No'). The TotalCharges column initially contained some empty string entries which were treated as zero before converting the column to a numeric type. This dataset provides a foundational set of attributes commonly analyzed in telecom churn prediction to understand the characteristics of customers who leave versus those who remain with the service provider.
